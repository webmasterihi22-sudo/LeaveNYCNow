Article I - NYC Budget Crisis

New York City's $5 Billion Black Hole: How the City Went Broke

Mayor Mamdani inherited a city already hemorrhaging red ink. His plans will make it catastrophically worse.

New York City begins the Mamdani era with a $5 billion structural budget deficit per the Independent Budget Office. Moody's has downgraded its financial outlook to negative. The city's top 1% of earners pay approximately 43% of all city income taxes - meaning every wealthy resident who moves to Florida takes an outsized share of city revenue with them.

Mamdani's spending agenda piles onto this fragile base: $800 million per year for free buses, multi-billion universal childcare, and $70 billion in housing bonds - all requiring state cooperation that Governor Hochul has already partially rejected. Budget experts warn of stark choices ahead: slash services or blow past the city's legal debt ceiling.

Article II - Property Tax Hikes

The Hidden Tax Bomb: Mamdani's Racial Equity Property Plan and What It Means for You

The mayor's plan to shift property tax burdens toward "richer and Whiter neighborhoods" has profound implications for outer borough families.

Mayor Mamdani's policy brief "Stop the Squeeze on NYC Homeowners" explicitly describes plans to shift tax burdens from outer borough homeowners to "more expensive homes in richer and Whiter neighborhoods." That language triggered an immediate review from the Trump DOJ's Civil Rights division.

DOJ Alert - Federal Review Underway

DOJ Assistant Attorney General Harmeet Dhillon stated she would "review" the plan as potentially "fishy/illegal." A formal challenge could trigger multi-year litigation at enormous cost to a city already in fiscal crisis.

Many outer borough homeowners - particularly in Flushing, Bayside, Bay Ridge, and South Shore Staten Island - have seen values rise to $800K-$1.5M. Under market-value taxation, these homeowners could actually see taxes rise. Small landlords face a triple threat: rent freeze cuts revenue, property taxes rise, minimum wage hike raises maintenance costs.

New York City receives approximately $7 billion annually in federal aid. If the DOJ determines NYC's equity programs violate federal anti-discrimination law, the city could lose that funding - a catastrophe that would dwarf the existing $5B structural deficit.

Article III - Race and Equity Agenda

Governing by Race: How Mamdani's Equity Agenda Is Dividing NYC and Inviting Federal Intervention

The Preliminary Citywide Racial Equity Plan has put NYC on a collision course with the Trump DOJ.

No modern NYC mayor has placed race as explicitly at the heart of governance as Zohran Mamdani. His Preliminary Citywide Racial Equity Plan, released in early 2026, is funded at $10.2 million annually - a 42% increase from prior spending - and directs every city agency to assess the racial impact of all budget and hiring decisions.

Mamdani also proposes replacing the NYPD with a new "Department of Community Safety." The restructuring has no proven model in any major American city. Minneapolis and Portland tried similar approaches with mixed to negative safety outcomes. Residents of high-crime neighborhoods - disproportionately Black and Latino New Yorkers - have raised serious concerns about the transition period.
